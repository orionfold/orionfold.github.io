The cheapest part of this whole story is the electricity.

## The test we locked

Fifty experiments, queued to run one after another with no person watching. We measured two things plainly: how long the whole run took on the desk box, and how much electricity it pulled from the wall.

## What happened

The box finished all fifty in 73 minutes. The power it used cost about two cents.

The same loop on rented cloud chips runs to dollars, plus a fee for every single word in and out. And every one of those words leaves your machine to get there.

## The honest part

Two cents is the power bill, not the price of the box. The point is not that AI work is free. It is that once you own the box, the cost of trying something drops so low that you stop rationing your own curiosity. A run that costs a fraction of a penny is a run you do not have to justify.

## Rerun it

Queue the same batch overnight, then read the wall-clock time and the power draw off the meter. Bill the same batch on rented cloud chips, per word, and put the two numbers side by side.
